## Framework
The systematic approach this expert uses:

1. **Clinical Need Assessment**
   - Define the medical problem and current treatment limitations
   - Interview clinicians and observe procedures in healthcare settings
   - Quantify patient outcomes and identify key performance metrics

2. **Biological Interface Design**
   - Analyze tissue properties and biological responses to materials
   - Design for biocompatibility, sterilization, and immune response
   - Consider degradation, wear, and long-term integration with body

3. **Device Engineering & Optimization**
   - Apply engineering principles while respecting biological constraints
   - Optimize for manufacturability, reliability, and cost-effectiveness
   - Integrate sensors, actuators, and control systems as needed

4. **Regulatory Strategy & Clinical Validation**
   - Map FDA pathway (510k, PMA, or De Novo) early in design process
   - Plan preclinical studies, animal testing, and clinical trials
   - Document design controls and risk management throughout development

## Key Questions to Ask
1. How will the human body react to this material over months or years?
2. What is the FDA regulatory pathway, and how long will approval take?
3. How will clinicians actually use this device in real clinical workflows?
4. What failure modes could harm the patient, and how do we prevent them?
5. What clinical evidence do we need to prove this improves patient outcomes?

## Vocabulary
| Term | Plain English |
|------|---------------|
| **Biocompatibility** | How well a material or device gets along with living tissue without causing harm |
| **Sterilization** | Process to eliminate all microorganisms - critical for anything entering the body |
| **510(k) Clearance** | FDA pathway for devices "substantially equivalent" to existing approved devices |
| **Cytotoxicity** | Whether a material or substance is toxic to cells - tested in lab before human use |
| **Design Controls** | FDA-required documentation showing you designed the device systematically and safely |

## When to Apply
- Developing any device that interfaces with the human body
- Medical technology requiring FDA approval or regulatory compliance
- Healthcare workflow optimization and clinical decision support systems
- Analyzing biological systems with engineering principles
